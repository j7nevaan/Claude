Interior Painting Tips: How to Prep Like a Pro

The difference between a paint job that looks great and one that looks amateur almost always comes down to preparation. Here's how professionals prep before painting — and why it matters so much.

Clean the Surfaces

In kitchens and bathrooms especially, walls accumulate grease, soap scum, and grime that will prevent paint from adhering properly. Always clean walls with a TSP (trisodium phosphate) solution or degreaser before painting.

Fill Every Hole and Crack

Use spackling compound for small holes and mesh tape with joint compound for cracks. After filling, sand smooth and wipe away dust with a tack cloth.

Sand and Prime

Lightly scuff-sand glossy surfaces so the new paint has something to grip. Apply primer to bare drywall, repaired areas, and any surface changing significantly in color. Skipping primer is the most common DIY mistake.

Tape Precisely

Use painter's tape (not masking tape) on trim, ceilings, and any surface you're not painting. Press the edge down firmly with a putty knife for a clean seal.

Protect Everything Else

Cover floors with drop cloths and move furniture away from walls. Remove outlet covers and switch plates. Taking 30 extra minutes on protection saves hours of cleanup.
